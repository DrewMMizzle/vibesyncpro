Nice clean foundation here — the minimalism is working in your favor. Here's how to push it to state-of-the-art:
Typography
The headline is good but generic. Consider a tighter line-height and a slight negative letter-spacing (−0.02em to −0.04em) on the display type — premium SaaS sites like Linear and Vercel do this and it reads as intentional craft. Also, the subhead gray is a bit flat; try a warmer neutral or slightly blue-tinted gray to feel less "default."
Hero Section
You have no visual anchor below the headline — just a CTA button floating in space. The best landing pages today use one of: a live product screenshot/mockup with a subtle drop shadow and slight tilt (3–5°), a short looping demo video embedded inline, or an animated diagram showing the branch-sync concept in motion. Right now the space between the CTA and the feature cards is dead air.
The CTA Button
The black pill button is solid, but consider adding a very subtle hover state — a slight scale (1.02) or a soft glow/shadow pulse. It signals interactivity before the click. Also the GitHub icon could be slightly larger relative to the text.
Feature Cards
The three cards are too uniform and too quiet. Modern approaches: give them a very slight glassmorphism treatment (backdrop-filter blur with 1px border), or use a subtle gradient border on hover. The icons are too small and too gray — size them up and give them a bit more personality. Also consider adding a one-line "so what" outcome stat under each description (e.g., "Know the moment a branch falls behind" → "Catches drift in < 30 seconds").
Spacing & Layout
The section between the hero CTA and the cards needs either a product visual or a tight social proof strip — logos of tools it connects to (Replit, Claude Code, GitHub) or a "used by X developers" micro-stat. This is the single biggest conversion gap on the page right now.
Color & Depth
Pure white (#FFFFFF) backgrounds feel slightly sterile now. The trending move is an off-white base — #FAFAFA or a barely-warm #F9F8F6 — which makes the white cards pop as elevated surfaces. Pair that with a very faint radial gradient in the hero (light gray blooming from center).
Navigation
"Log in" alone in the top right is lonely. Add a secondary "Book a demo" or "See how it works" text link next to it, and consider pinning the nav with a frosted glass effect on scroll — it signals production quality immediately.
The biggest single ROI move: drop a product screenshot or animated GIF of the actual dashboard between the CTA button and the feature cards. That dead space is where you're losing visitors.
